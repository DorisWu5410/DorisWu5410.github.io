About

Dataset

Info about dataset: features, range, median, etc

The data set that we used includes information of Hass avocado sales from January 2015 to November 2020 throughout the United States. For each recorded date, the average price, total volume sold, total number of bags of each type sold, and the region were provided. The price of an avocado usually fell between $0.44 and $3.25 with an average price around $1.40. An average of 850,644 avocados were sold in each region. Lastly, the region that sold the most avocados was the West, selling an average 3,215,323 each day.



Goal

Our Mission

We are attempting to enable our project (AVO) to learn from past data and trends to accurately predict avocado prices. AVO will help sellers, manufacturers, and consumers all around. Sellers in particular will be able to identify the optimal price for avocados and maximize their exports. Consumers will also benefit from locating the cheapest areas to purchase avocados and considering the average price of one of our most beloved fruits.

    Price Visualization in California

    The neural network model expresses the price of avocados in a given state using the avocado data set. Using the data from the previous thirty days, it predicts the price of avocados for the next month. The blue line of the graph shows the actual price of avocados which is used as reference to evaluate the accuracy of the model. The orange segment shows the data the model used for training while the green line is actual prediction.

  • XGBoosting Tree

    Utilizing Total Volume, Bags, Type, Region, and Date to Predict Avocado Prices

    This is a XGBoost regression model for our avocado data set. Each branch of my “tree” represents a region/location and possesses a “leaf” containing the prediction for the price of avocados for that region (if the price will be greater than or less than 1 dollar) Essentially, it takes the average prices of all avocados in that specific region and price range and outputs its prediction based on that data. As of right now, I have cut it down to make a tree that only includes regions that have prices under fifty cents an avocado. Once it makes the prediction for a region, it moves on to the next “branch” or region and repeats the process.

  • LinearRegression Model

    Using Total Volume and Date to Predict Price

    LinearRegression is a simple model that takes in data points and generates a line that best fits the data. It can take in multiple factors into consideration when creating a line. I used LinearRegression to take both total volume and date into consideration when predicting the price. I also use LinearRegression to individually compare date to average price and total volume to average price. The 3 dimensional graph below shows the predicted prices of avocados generated by my model. The two graphs below individually compare total volume and date to average prices.
